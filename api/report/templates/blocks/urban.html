Urban growth

The FUTURES urban growth model predicts the likelihood that an area will urbanize at every decade from 2020 to 2100. Developed areas from the 2019 National Land Cover Database serve as the baseline for current urban areas. The model simulates landscape change based on demand for development, local development suitability factors, and a random urban patch-growing algorithm. It considers trends in population growth and building footprints, environmental drivers like distance to floodplain, slope, and available amenities, and even social factors like reluctance to sell in certain areas. The probability of urbanization for each area reflects how many times it urbanized out of 50 model runs. Darker reds indicate higher probability of urbanization.
